Control/ Responsibility
Overall responsibility to build, maintain & control GS reserve spares will rest with GHQ, EME directorate (Equipment Divisions). Said spares stock will not be issued/ utilized without prior permission of GHQ, EME directorate.

Procurement
Procurement planning will be done by EME directorate (preferably using Tier-1). While planning procurement, it will be ensured that build-up of Sustenance stock is preferred over GS Reserve Stock.

Stocking


a. Selection of Spares
EME directorate will select vehicles/ equipment, for which GS Reserve is to be maintained. It will be based on criticality of vehicles/ equipment in war/ operations (including equipment of dependent formations which will be in respective AOR during war, only). Selection criteria have been explained at Annex ‘B’.

b. Stocking Quantity
GHQ, Log directorate will specify number of days for which GS reserve is to be maintained. Accordingly, quantity will be finalized by EME directorate. Calculation methodology for ‘GS Reserve Spares’ has been explained at Annex ‘B’.

a. Stocking Location
Stocking will be done at SDs/ EME Battalions/ independent workshops, keeping in view the disposition of vehicles/ equipment in respective area of responsibility (AOR). Distribution plan will be issued by EME directorate.

b. Storage Instructions

1. ‘GS Reserve Spares’ will be stored separately from the ‘Sustenance spares’.
2. Each rack will be dedicated for a specific model of a particular make of vehicle/ equipment e.g. separate store bin/ rack will be earmarked of all models of B Vehicles ex Toyota.
3. Further bifurcation will be done on basis of system/ sub system of the vehicle/ equipment.
4. Guide Map to this methodology shall be displayed in each Spares Stocking Shed.

c. Preservation/ Turnover Instructions
 Following will be ensured:-
1. Keeping in view the shelf life of items, turn over with sustenance stocks will be ensured by respective stocking agency i.e. workshops and SDs.
2. The items, which could not be turned over due to any reason, will be reported to EME directorate at least 6x months prior to expiry of shelf life. In turn EME directorate will ensure remedial measures including transfer to other SDs etc.
3. If an item could not be consumed due to any reason, it will be disposed-off by declaring it BER through a condemnation board by SD. Recoupment of these items will be covered under ‘Replenishment Demands’.
3. Report covering turn over details and items approaching expiry of shelf life will be submitted by each CEME/ OC/ SD on 1st of February and August each year.

d. Biannual Review
EME directorate (concerned Equipment Divisions) will ensure biannual review of the spares stocked as GS reserve. Deletion or addition of spares will be approved by DGEME basing on recommendations of Equipment Divisions, EME directorate.
